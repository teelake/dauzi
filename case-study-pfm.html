Challenge

A West African government was facing persistent budget execution challenges: delayed cash releases, limited visibility into spending across MDAs, and weak internal controls. This led to project delays, underspending in critical areas, and audit findings that eroded donor confidence.

Approach

We worked with the Ministry of Finance and select MDAs to:

  • Establish a monthly cash forecasting process that linked budget allocations to actual inflows
  • Design and implement a budget transparency portal that published monthly execution reports
  • Strengthen internal controls through process mapping, risk assessments, and control matrices
  • Deliver targeted training for finance teams on budget execution and reporting

Outcomes

  • 90%+ budget execution rates in priority MDAs within 18 months
  • Reduced cash release delays from 45 days to under 14 days on average
  • Public transparency: Monthly budget execution reports published on government portal
  • Improved audit compliance: Reduced audit findings by 60% in targeted MDAs

Client: Ministry of Finance, supported by World Bank

Duration: 24 months

Scope: Public Financial Management, Budget Transparency, Internal Controls
